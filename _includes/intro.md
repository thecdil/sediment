## Beginning

Embedded in Glen Canyon's sandstone walls and muddy shorelines is a vibrant archive of stories. Fossilized tracks, ephemeral footprints, and microbial burrows trace the rhythms and pulses of the canyon's biotic life. Wind-cut furrows and river-carved chasms recount ancient interplays between water, wind, and land. Petroglyph tapestries, mining scars, and a seven-hundred-foot-tall concrete dam catalog diverse and sometimes conflictory relationships between humans and the natural world. Together, these sedimented records reveal how Glen Canyonâ€™s various agents have interacted with the landscape--and with each other, often across seemingly disjointed times and spaces. 

*Sedimentation* is a collection of these stories, excavated from Glen Canyon's sedimentary archive. 

*Sedimentation* offers many pathways for you to explore Glen Canyon and its stories. Pick a *tributary* to flow through curated histories of the Canyon's biota, humans, atmosphere, water, or land. Drift along the *eddies* to navigate Glen Canyon through interlinked story fragments. Roam the *map* to discover histories by location. Check out the photograph *collections* to see a visual catalog of Glen Canyon's earthly archive. Or, keep scrolling to follow a sediment particle as it encounters the various human and nonhuman entities that have left their marks on Glen Canyon.
