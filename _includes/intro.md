## Beginning

Embedded in Glen Canyon's sandstone walls and sandy shorelines is a vibrant archive of stories. Fossilized tracks, ephemeral footprints, and microbial burrows trace the flourishings and diminishments of the canyon's biotic life. Wind-cut furrows, river-carved chasms, and elaborately swirled rock formations recount ancient interplays between water, wind, and land. Petroglyph tapestries, gouged trails, dynamite-blasted mining scars, and a seven-hundred foot tall concrete dam catalog varied and sometimes conflictory relationships between humans and the natural world. *Sedimentation* is a collection of these stories, excavated from Glen Canyon's sedimentary archive.
