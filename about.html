About the Project

*Sedimentation* represents sediment around the site of Glen Canyon in its multitude of formulations, interactions, and movements. Sediment is often overlooked in the western cultural imaginary, perhaps because it is mundane, or because oftentimes it is out of sight, or operating at scales too small or too large to witness.

*Sedimentation* hopes to provide a platform for viewers to engage with and better understand sediment, to trace its connections through the ecosystem, and to attune to its liveliness.

About the Colorado River & Glen Canyon Dam

The Colorado River has shaped the landscape of the American Southwest for millions of years. The Glen Canyon Dam, completed in 1963, fundamentally altered the river's natural flow patterns and sediment transport.

Before the dam, the river carried massive amounts of sediment that built beaches, supported riparian vegetation, and maintained the natural ecosystem. The dam now traps this sediment, creating clear water releases that have transformed the downstream environment.

About Sediment

Sediment exists in many forms - from microscopic clay particles to large boulders. It can be part of the land, water, or air; it is not fixed in its form, it exceeds and disturbs cartographic boundaries, and is impossible to contain either physically or in representation.

Understanding sediment requires recognizing its agency - its ability to shape landscapes, create habitats, and connect distant places through its movement.
